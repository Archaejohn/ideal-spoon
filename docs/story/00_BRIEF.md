# Creative Brief — "Aetherbound" (working title)

> **This is the anchor.** Every writer, worldbuilder, and reviewer builds on this. It fixes the premise, tone, cast skeleton, structure, and the branch/ending targets so parallel work stays coherent. Flesh it out richly — do not contradict it without flagging the Owner.

## Logline
On a dying world of floating islands, a 14-year-old lamp-keeper who can *hear* the fading magic must decide what to do with the sleeping machine-heart that powers everything — before a desperate empire drains it to lift their own cities and let the rest of the world fall.

## Genre & blend
Epic **fantasy with buried sci-fi** (the "magic," *aether*, is the residue of a vanished precursor civilization's technology — espers/magitek-style, but wholly original). Hopeful, adventurous, wondrous. Storyline weight of a classic 16-bit ensemble RPG (FF III/VI feel): a large heartfelt cast, a mid-game world-changing cataclysm, branching/merging paths, and multiple endings — **100% original IP**.

## Audience & tone (hard constraints)
- For a **10-year-old reading at a 6th-grade level**. Clear, vivid, not babyish.
- **No profanity, no graphic violence, no mature themes.** Stakes are real (loss, sacrifice, a dying world) but handled with warmth and hope. Villains are sincere and tragic, not cruel for cruelty's sake. Death exists but is shown gently (fading to light, "the Long Quiet"), never gory.
- Themes: **stewardship vs. exploitation; found family; the courage of small people; that saving a world means changing how we live together, not one magic fix; hope.**

## The world (skeleton — Worldbuilder enriches in 01_WORLD.md)
- **Aetheris:** a world of **skylands** (floating islands) above a misted **Underland** surface. Long ago the **Skyborn** (precursor civilization) raised the skylands using **aether** drawn from the **Wellspring**, an ancient machine-heart deep in the world. The Skyborn vanished in the **Sundering**. Since then, aether slowly fades; skylands sink; people crowd onto the few that remain aloft.
- **Aether** is treated as magic. Most people only use it stored in **wellstones** (lamps, ships, tools). A rare few, **Resonants**, can *hear and shape* it directly (the "Song").
- **The Hollow:** as aether drains from a place, husks called **Hollow** stir — creatures and machines emptied of resonance. They are a spreading danger, not demons.
- Travel is by **skyship** between islands; the sea of mist below is dangerous and half-mythologized.

## Factions (skeleton)
- **The Ascendancy** — a mighty sky-nation. Facing the decline, they plan to drain the Wellspring to lift their own cities ever higher and outlast the fall — saving *some* by dooming the rest. Sincere, not cartoonish.
- **The Lantern Order** — a faith that venerates the Skyborn and tends the wellstone "lamps"; believes the world must simply be kept lit and prayed over. Denial vs. evidence.
- **Free skylands & sky-folk** — independent towns, traders, smugglers, salvagers of Skyborn relics.
- **The Skyborn / the Warden** — the vanished makers, and the dormant intelligence inside the Wellspring (the **Warden / Heartmind**) — the story's central mystery.

## Cast skeleton (Character Writer deepens in 02_CHARACTERS.md; Plot Architect may use all of these)
Core party (target ~6 playable, FF-VI-style ensemble; a couple optional via branches):
1. **Wren** — 14, apprentice **aether-tender** on a small dying skyland; a hidden **Resonant** who can hear the Song. Arc: timid keeper → the one who decides the Wellspring's fate. (Heart of the story; courage/hope.)
2. **Captain Sable Vance** — pragmatic, wry **skyship captain/salvager** who lost her home island to the sinking. Arc: cynic → believer. (Warmth + comic edge; early mentor.)
3. **Tam Brightgear** — Wren's best friend, a young **inventor** who builds gadgets from Skyborn relics. Arc: reckless tinkerer → responsible. (Comic relief + tech/utility.)
4. **Mira** — a young healer of the **Lantern Order**. Arc: faith vs. evidence; learns to hold both. (Support; moral compass.)
5. **Kestrel** — a defecting **Ascendancy sky-knight** with a strict code. Arc: duty → conscience; redemption. (Recruitable via a branch; FF-VI-Celes energy.)
6. **Bramble** — a small **Skyborn construct/automaton** Wren awakens; carries fragmented memories of the makers. Arc: tool → person; the world's living conscience. (Lore + heart; non-human party member.)
7. *(Optional, branch-recruited)* **Rookwise** — an exiled elder scholar of forbidden Skyborn lore.

Antagonists:
- **High Regent Corvus Thane** — leader of the Ascendancy. Tragic, sincere; believes hoarding is the only mercy. Not evil — *wrong, and grieving.*
- **Marrow** — Thane's enforcer; a **fallen Resonant** who lost their Song. Wren's dark foil — what Wren could become without hope.
- **The Warden (Heartmind)** — not a villain: the sleeping mind in the Wellspring. The final "boss" is really a **choice**.

## Structure (Plot Architect details in 03_MAIN_STORY.md)
Four acts, with a **mid-game cataclysm** that darkens the world (a "Second Sundering"), echoing the two-halves structure of the classics:
- **Act I — The Keeper's Lamp:** village life → Ascendancy harvesters arrive → Wren's Song awakens → the village lamp fails → flee on Sable's ship → first companions → reach the hub skytown **Hollowgate**.
- **Act II — The Sinking Roads:** travel the skylands; learn the truth of aether and the Wellspring; **branch points** (which skyland/faction to aid first); recruit Kestrel; confront Marrow. Climax: the Ascendancy forces a partial Wellspring drain → **the Fading** reshapes the map and scatters the party.
- **Act III — The Fading World:** darker, post-cataclysm; reunite the scattered party, side quests open, face consequences; converging paths; reach the Wellspring's gate.
- **Act IV — The Wellspring:** the Warden; the final **choice** → endings.

## Branch/merge & endings (Plot Architect details in 04_BRANCHES_ENDINGS.md)
- At least **3 meaningful diverge/merge points** in Acts II–III (e.g., which island to save first; spare vs. resist Kestrel's pursuit; trust the Lantern Order's secret vs. expose it). Paths **merge** back so the game stays buildable.
- **Multiple distinct endings**, keyed to the final Wellspring choice + cumulative choices:
  - **A — "The Shared Dawn"** (share/redistribute aether; best/hardest; requires uniting factions): hopeful.
  - **B — "The Long Quiet"** (let the Wellspring sleep forever; aether ends, the world settles to a gentler, lower life): bittersweet.
  - **C — "The Ascendant Throne"** (Thane's path prevails or you take the power): cautionary, not graphic.
  - **D — "The Wandering Star"** (secret): wake the Warden as a partner and set out to find the vanished Skyborn.

## Canon rulings (Owner — binding; resolve worldbuilder's open questions)
1. **The Skyborn were one people.** In the Sundering they did not all die — most **departed** (stepped out of the world to somewhere else); some were lost. In-world it stays a mystery, but canon is: one people, mostly *elsewhere*. This powers the secret Ending D ("find the vanished Skyborn").
2. **Resonants are born, rare; they cannot be manufactured.** The Ascendancy **conscripts** natural Resonants and tries to *force/augment* the Song with wellstone rigs — which fails tragically, causing Songsickness and hollowing. **This is exactly what broke Marrow** (a conscripted Resonant burned out by the Ascendancy's rigs). Strong, non-graphic moral stakes for the Cinderworks.
3. **"The Long Quiet" double meaning is intentional** — the gentle word for death *and* the name of Ending B (the Wellspring sleeping forever, the world settling into a gentler Long Quiet). Keep the thematic rhyme.

## Naming guardrails (originality)
No names, places, items, or plot lifted from existing IP. If a name feels close to a known franchise, change it. Keep names pronounceable for a 10-year-old.
