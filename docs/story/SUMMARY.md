# Aetherbound — One-Page Story Summary (for sign-off)

**Genre:** Original epic fantasy with buried sci-fi (the "magic," *aether*, is the residue of a vanished precursor civilization's technology). Storyline weight of a classic 16-bit ensemble RPG; fully original IP. Age-appropriate for a 10-year-old at a 6th-grade reading level.

**Logline:** On the dying sky-world of Aetheris, where floating islands sink one quiet inch at a time into the mist below, a timid 14-year-old lamp-keeper named **Wren** discovers she is a **Resonant** — one of the rare few who can *hear* the fading magic, the **Song**. When the mighty **Ascendancy** comes to drain her village's last light, she flees with a found family of misfits and chases the secret of the **Wellspring**, the world's sleeping machine-heart — only to learn the Ascendancy means to drain it to lift their own cities and let the rest of the sky fall.

**The heart of it:** This isn't good vs. evil. It's **hoarding vs. sharing in a dying world.** The villain, **High Regent Thane**, is sincere and grieving — he believes saving a few is the only mercy left. The hero's dark mirror, **Marrow**, is a fellow Resonant who lost hope. The question the whole game builds toward is not *who* lives, but *how the world chooses to live together.*

**Cast (found family):** Wren (the listening heart) · **Sable** the wry skyship captain who lost her home · **Tam** the excitable young inventor · **Mira** the gentle Order healer torn between faith and evidence · **Kestrel** the defecting sky-knight · **Bramble** the small awakened machine who becomes a person · (optional) **Rookwise** the exiled scholar.

**Shape (four acts, FF-VI-style two halves):**
- **Act I — The Keeper's Lamp:** home, Wren's Song awakens, the flight from Meadowmoor, first companions, the hub city Hollowgate.
- **Act II — The Sinking Roads:** travel the skylands, learn the truth, recruit allies, confront Marrow — then **fail** to stop the Ascendancy's partial drain: the **Second Sundering** breaks the map and scatters the party.
- **Act III — The Fading World:** a greyed, post-cataclysm world; reunite the family, face the consequences, resolve the Order's secret, converge on the Wellspring.
- **Act IV — The Wellspring:** the sleeping **Warden**, a final reckoning with Thane and Marrow, and **the choice.**

**Branching & endings (diverge → merge, fully mapped & deterministic):** four branch points (which dying island to save; whether the knight Kestrel defects; expose or protect the Order's secret; recruit the scholar Rookwise) feed a "unity" model that gates **four distinct endings** — **A** *The Shared Dawn* (share the light; best, hardest, must unite the world), **B** *The Long Quiet* (let the Wellspring sleep; bittersweet peace), **C** *The Ascendant Throne* (take the power for a few; cautionary), and the secret **D** *The Wandering Star* (wake the Warden as a partner and sail off the edge of the sky to find the vanished makers).

**Where it soars (highs make the lows land):**
- **Triumph & wonder** — Act I ends on an exhilarating *getaway* and Wren's first laugh, not just a goodbye; first flight is pure wonder.
- **A joyful peak before the fall** — the whole found family throws a lantern-lit **Relighting festival**: dancing, sun-bread, Bramble's first festival ("I did not know light was allowed to be only *glad*"), Kestrel's one reluctant dance ("tactically unnecessary"). Then the cataclysm hits — and it hurts *because* you just danced.
- **Light wins in the grey** — Act III has real victories: a town relit to cheers, a Hollow swarm gently calmed.
- **It's actually fun** — a running comedic engine: Tam's gloriously unreliable inventions, Bramble haltingly learning to make a joke, Sable's deadpan. Endings A and D pay off in sky-wide celebration.

**Where the heart lands:**
- A **personal loss** — Wren's mentor **Keeper Pell** keeps the last lamp lit by hand so a refugee convoy can escape, and fades gently into "the Long Quiet." His ritual ("two taps, a breath — *all's well*") and the lamp he gave her carry through to the ending.
- A **real family rift** — cynical Sable and hopeful Wren actually break over "hope is a tax I can't pay," and heal — the whole theme fought between two people who love each other.
- A **climax felt through a person** — in three of four endings, **Bramble** gives the last of itself to complete or quiet the Song ("I am not afraid"); only the secret ending brings Bramble home whole.

**Scope delivered:** 4 acts, ~50 story beats (ledgered as an engine contract), 4 branch/merge points, 4 endings, 16 side quests (6 companion + 8 world + 2 mastery arcs), and production-ready scripts for the marquee scenes.

**Content & quality:** Independently reviewed — *approve with minor fixes*; all fixes applied, including re-staging one scene to remove any self-harm reading. No profanity, no gore; loss is always shown as light, never blood; villains are tragic, not cruel.
