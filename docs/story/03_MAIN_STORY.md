## Synopsis

On the dying sky-world of Aetheris, where floating islands sink one quiet inch at a time into the mist below, a timid fourteen-year-old lamp-keeper named **Wren** discovers she is a **Resonant** — one of the rare few who can *hear* the fading magic, the **Song**. When the mighty **Ascendancy** comes to drain her village's last light, Wren flees aboard a salvager's skyship and falls in with a found family of misfits: the wry captain **Sable**, her inventor friend **Tam**, the Order healer **Mira**, a small awakened Skyborn machine called **Bramble**, and — if she earns it — a defecting sky-knight, **Kestrel**. Chasing the secret of the **Wellspring**, the world's sleeping machine-heart, they learn the terrible truth: the Ascendancy means to drain it to lift their own cities and let the rest of the sky fall. They are too late to stop the first, partial drain — the **Second Sundering** — which shatters the map and scatters the party across a greying world. Reunited and tested, they descend at last to the Wellspring and its dormant guardian, the **Warden**, where Wren must decide not just who lives, but how the whole world will learn to live together. The ending the world receives is the one Wren and her friends have earned along the way.

## ACT I — The Keeper's Lamp

**Goal:** Establish home, awaken Wren's Song, force the flight from Meadowmoor, gather the first companions, and arrive at the hub of the world.

**Locations:** Meadowmoor (Wren's home) → the Sky-Roads → Hollowgate (hub skytown) → the Skyborn wreck beneath Hollowgate.

**Party recruited:** **Sable Vance** (captain), **Tam Brightgear** (inventor), **Bramble** (Skyborn construct).

### Key beats (in order)

1. **A1-01 — The lamp-bell.** Dawn on Meadowmoor. Wren and her teacher **Keeper Pell** tend the village's failing great-lamp. Tutorial: Wren *Listens* and hears the Song thin and faltering. Gentle, sleepy, beloved — the world worth saving, shown first.
2. **A1-02 — Wisps at the edge.** Faint **Hollow** drift in from the dimming rim of the island. Wren's best friend **Tam** arrives with a clattering homemade ward-gadget; together they shoo the wisps off. Tam introduced; first combat tutorial.
3. **A1-03 — The harvesters arrive.** An Ascendancy harvester-ship docks. A polite, joyless officer announces Meadowmoor's great-stone will be "requisitioned for the greater good." Foreshadows Thane's triage; the villagers' fear.
4. **A1-04 — The Song awakens.** During the standoff the over-stressed great-lamp gutters and dies. Without thinking, Wren *Steadies* it — relighting it for one impossible minute in front of everyone. She is revealed, publicly, as a **Resonant**.
5. **A1-05 — Conscription.** Ascendancy doctrine: Resonants are too valuable to leave free. The officer moves to take Wren. Keeper Pell stalls them; Wren is hurried toward the docks, where the salvager **Sable Vance** is loading scrap. Sable introduced (reluctant, wry).
6. **A1-06 — Leaving home.** Wren, Tam, and Sable flee aboard Sable's ship, **the Driftwing**. Keeper Pell stays behind to keep the lamp lit, pressing the **Keeper's Lamp** (a special wellstone) into Wren's hands. *Emotional turn:* the cost of the first goodbye.
   - **A1-06b — The keeper's habit.** That first night on the Sky-Roads the *Driftwing*'s keel-stone runs nervous and thin. Wren settles it the way Pell taught her — *two taps on the cradle, one slow breath, and the words "the lamp is lit, we're aloft, all's well."* It is the first light she has ever kept without him, and she understands, quietly, that the habit is how she gets to carry him along. *Plants Pell's ritual, his saying, and the Keeper's Lamp as the personal thread Acts III–IV pay off.*
7. **A1-07 — The Sky-Roads.** First voyage; tutorial of skyship travel and the wind-roads. A Hollow swarm attacks mid-route; Wren's *Listen* spots the danger early and saves the ship. Sable's cynicism begins to crack.
8. **A1-08 — Hollowgate.** Arrival at the patchwork hub-city built around a beached Skyborn cathedral-ship. Wonder beat. Sable is broke and owes the salvagers' guild; she takes a job diving the old wreck beneath the city.
9. **A1-09 — Into the wreck.** The party descends into the humming Skyborn hull-undermarket. Tam is giddy at the relics. Deep inside they find a small, dormant construct curled like a sleeping cat.
10. **A1-10 — Awakening Bramble.** Wren *Shapes* aether into the construct and wakes **Bramble** (Shape tutorial). Bramble speaks in fragments and hums the Song's motif; it carries broken Skyborn memories. Bramble joins.
11. **A1-11 — The Sleepless Crane.** Waking Bramble stirs the wreck's guardian husk, a great hollowed crane-construct. **Boss: the Sleepless Crane.** The party fights free and escapes upward.
12. **A1-12 — The shape of the danger.** Safe above, Bramble's fragments make one thing clear: the world is dying *far faster* than anyone admits, and every lamp draws on one root — the **Wellspring**. Wren resolves to find the truth. A cloaked figure watches from the crowd: **Marrow**. *Hook to Act II.*

**Boss / encounter highlights:** Hollow wisps (Meadowmoor) · Hollow swarm (Sky-Roads) · **the Sleepless Crane** (Act I boss, the wreck).

**Emotional turn:** From a small safe life to a wide, frightening, wondrous world — and the dawning realization that the quiet music only Wren can hear may be the thing that decides everything.

## ACT II — The Sinking Roads

**Goal:** Travel the skylands, uncover the truth of aether and the Wellspring, gather allies, confront Marrow — and fail to stop the Ascendancy's partial drain, which triggers the cataclysm that scatters the party.

**Locations:** Hollowgate → **Glasswastes OR Verdance** (BR1) → Hollowgate → Cinderworks → the Wellspring's outer gate-relay.

**Party recruited:** **Mira** (Lantern Order healer) · **Kestrel** (defecting sky-knight — via **BR2**).

### Key beats (in order)

1. **A2-01 — The great lamp.** At Hollowgate's Lantern Order shrine, Wren's Songsickness flares; a young healer, **Mira**, tends her. The Order calmly denies the Fading is real. Mira introduced; faith-vs-evidence seeded.
2. **A2-02 — Two dying islands. ⟶ BRANCH BR1.** Bramble's map-fragment points to two failing skylands, each holding a Skyborn memory-shard. The Driftwing can only reach one before the other slips into the mist. **Branch point BR1:** sail to the **Glasswastes** or to **Verdance** first.
3. **A2-03 — (Glasswastes, BR1·A).** The mirror-glass desert that rings like a bell. **Glass Hollow** scatter the light. The party shelters refugees and recovers the **Departure shard** — a Bramble memory of the Skyborn *stepping out of the world*. Mira joins here, moved to act.
4. **A2-04 — (Verdance, BR1·B).** The overgrown jungle-island. Garden-constructs tidy travelers like weeds. The party frees trapped villagers and recovers the **Promise shard** — a Bramble memory of the Warden and a promise the makers made. Mira joins here, moved to act.
5. **A2-05 — The road not taken (merge).** Back at Hollowgate. Word arrives that the island they *didn't* reach has slipped into the mist. The party grieves; Bramble assembles the clues: the Ascendancy is already moving on the Wellspring. *(BR1 merges here.)*
6. **A2-06 — The Cinderworks.** The Ascendancy's grim furnace-island. The party sees conscripted Resonants worked past breaking on wellstone-rigs — Songsick, hollowing. The non-graphic moral horror that *broke Marrow* made plain. (Optional: free the conscripts — a unity choice.)
7. **A2-07 — The knight on the wind. ⟶ BRANCH BR2.** **Kestrel**, an Ascendancy sky-knight with a strict code, is sent to bring the Resonant in. **Branch point BR2:** *resist and evade* her, or *stand and show her the truth* of what the Cinderworks does to Resonants.
8. **A2-08 — Doubt (BR2 fork resolves first half).** If shown the truth, Kestrel's certainty cracks — her code was built on a lie about mercy. If evaded, she hardens into a relentless, sympathetic pursuer. Flag set for her recruitment window.
9. **A2-09 — Marrow at the pump.** At the great aether-pump the party confronts **Marrow**, Thane's fallen-Resonant enforcer — Wren's dark foil, the despair she could become. **Boss: Marrow.** Defeated but not destroyed, Marrow reveals Thane's plan: a "partial" Wellspring drain to lift the citadel-cities.
10. **A2-10 — Kestrel's choice (BR2 resolves).** With Marrow's reveal hanging in the air, Kestrel decides. If her doubt was earned, she **defects and joins the party**; if not, she lets them go but stays loyal — for now. *(BR2 merges here.)*
11. **A2-11 — Racing the drain.** The party rushes to the Wellspring's outer **gate-relay** to stop the harvest. They breach Ascendancy lines but arrive as the machinery is already spinning up. **High Regent Thane** appears — grieving, certain, immovable.
12. **A2-12 — The Second Sundering.** Thane triggers the partial drain. The world *lurches*: color drains toward grey, the Song screams and goes thin, and a ring of skylands shudders and falls. The shockwave tears **the Driftwing** apart in the air. **CLIMAX / cataclysm.**
13. **A2-13 — Scattered.** Wren plummets and wakes alone but for Bramble on a dim fragment of broken land, the sky changed and the party flung to the winds. *Emotional turn:* the lowest point — the world is breaking, the family is scattered, and it may already be too late. **End Act II.**

**Boss / encounter highlights:** Glass Hollow / garden-constructs (BR1 islands) · Tinplate patrols (Cinderworks) · **Marrow** (Act II boss) · the gate-relay set-piece (the drain).

**Emotional turn:** Hope meets the hard wall of consequence. The heroes do everything right and still cannot stop the disaster — the brief's "Second Sundering" hinge that breaks the world and the World-of-Ruin scatter that opens Act III.

## ACT III — The Fading World

**Goal:** Survive the greyed, post-cataclysm world, reunite the scattered family, face the consequences of Act II's choices, resolve the Lantern Order's secret, and converge — with whatever allies were earned — on the Wellspring's gate.

**Locations:** a broken skyland fragment → **The Hush** → Thornholt → Stillmere → the Wellspring gate.

**Party recruited / reunited:** Sable, Tam, Mira (reunited) · **Rookwise** (optional scholar — via **BR4**).

### Key beats (in order)

1. **A3-01 — After.** Wren and Bramble alone in a dim, half-Fading world where the Hollow are bold and the Song is nearly deaf. Tone shift to melancholy. Bramble steadies Wren when her courage fails. (Bramble's arc: tool → the world's conscience.)
2. **A3-02 — The Hush.** They cross to **The Hush**, a skyland nearly claimed by the Fading — a glimpse of what *all* the sky becomes if no one acts. Wren learns the kindest, hardest answer: to **re-tune** Hollow and calm them rather than fight. (Optional: rescue the survivors — a unity choice.)
   - **A3-02b — The keeper kept a light.** Among the last of The Hush's holdouts, Wren finds **Keeper Pell** — evacuated here when Meadowmoor was emptied, and now too frail to fly out, keeping the island's failing chapel-lamp lit by hand so stragglers can find their way to the boats. Reunion: warmth, his old sayings, the relief of a found-again father. *Emotional turn:* the loved one she thought lost, returned — briefly.
   - **A3-02c — Into the Long Quiet.** As the last survivors are carried off, the chapel-lamp will not hold for both the leaving and the staying. Pell chooses to keep it lit himself — a keeper's plain hand-craft, no Song — so no one is left in the dark, and fades gently into the **Long Quiet** as the light he tended outlasts him. Wren keeps his ritual at his side. *A gentle, permanent loss; the personal wound the finale answers.*
3. **A3-03 — The captain returns.** They find **Sable** running rescue-runs for refugees in a patched-together hull. Her arc lands: the cynic who lost her home now spends herself saving others. The party has a ship again.
   - **A3-03b — The tax of hope.** Raw from losing Pell, Wren insists they can still save *everyone*; Sable, who has now lost a home twice, snaps that "hope is a tax I can't pay" and that trying to save all of them is how you watch all of them sink. A real rift opens between the two people who anchor the family. *The game's theme — hoarding vs. sharing — fought out between two who love each other.*
   - **A3-03c — Worth the tax.** On a desperate rescue-run that should not work, Sable spends herself for strangers and it *holds* — and she admits, gruff and shaking, that Wren's stubborn hope is the only thing that ever got her to fly *toward* people instead of away. The rift reconciles; the captain chooses belief out loud. *Sable's arc lands through the family, not a speech.*
4. **A3-04 — Thornholt haven.** The salvager shantytown has become a crowded refugee haven. **Tam** is reunited here, churning out Hollow-wards and rescue gadgets; his recklessness has become responsibility.
5. **A3-05 — Mira's crisis.** **Mira** rejoins as the Lantern Order fractures under the disaster it swore could never come. Her faith is shaken; supporting her here (a small choice) keeps her trust through the chapter.
6. **A3-06 — The exile in the wreck. ⟶ BRANCH BR4.** Deep in Thornholt's relic-wrecks, the party can find and free **Rookwise**, an exiled scholar of forbidden Skyborn lore. **Branch point BR4 (optional recruit):** pursue and recruit Rookwise, or leave the exile be. Rookwise can reconstruct the Skyborn truth the lost island took with it.
7. **A3-07 — The road runs through Stillmere.** Bramble's shards and Rookwise's reading agree: the path to the Wellspring gate — and the gate-key — lies at **Stillmere**, the Lantern Order's monastery, which has guarded a secret for centuries.
8. **A3-08 — The Order's secret.** At Stillmere's mirror-reservoir the elders confess: the Order has *always known* the Skyborn did not die but **departed**, and that their still reservoir is a Skyborn relay hiding the gate-key. They buried the truth to protect the people's faith.
9. **A3-09 — Faith or evidence. ⟶ BRANCH BR3.** **Branch point BR3:** **expose** the Order's secret to the whole sky, or **protect** it. Either can be done with honor; each sets a different shape for the world's final unity (and for Mira's heart).
10. **A3-10 — Marrow in the grey.** Marrow returns — hunting, or perhaps fleeing the despair that hollowed them. A pivotal scene: Wren, who refused to become Marrow, can reach across the gap. Whether Marrow is moved depends on the hope shown all game. (Mini-boss or turning point.)
11. **A3-11 — The full drain.** Thane, desperate after the partial drain only quickened the Fading, commits to a **complete** drain. The party rallies every ally it has earned — sky-folk, Order, defectors — to reach the gate first. (Who answers the call depends on the unity built.)
12. **A3-12 — The family whole.** Full party reunited at the Wellspring gate. Using the Stillmere key, Bramble's memory, and Wren's Song, the gate opens. If recruited, Kestrel breaks the Ascendancy line so they can pass. *Emotional turn:* found family restored, descending together.
13. **A3-13 — The held breath.** The descent through the mist toward the Wellspring — the world holding its breath. Bramble's last memory surfaces; the central mystery sharpens to a single question. *(All branch flags now locked for the ending calculation.)* **End Act III.**

**Boss / encounter highlights:** bold Hollow & re-tuning set-pieces (The Hush) · refugee-defense at Thornholt · **Marrow** (the grey, A3-10) · the gate-breach at A3-12.

**Emotional turn:** From scattered despair to reunion and resolve — and the bittersweet weight of carrying everyone's choices down into the dark together.

## ACT IV — The Wellspring

**Goal:** Reach the Warden, face Thane and Marrow one last time, learn as much of the truth as was earned, and make the final choice that decides the world.

**Locations:** the descent → the **Wellspring** chamber (the machine-heart).

**Party:** the full earned roster.

### Key beats (in order)

1. **A4-01 — The machine-heart.** The vast Wellspring chamber: a sleeping engine of light below the mist, and within it the dormant **Warden (Heartmind)**. Awe and stillness.
2. **A4-02 — The grieving regent.** **Thane** arrives with **Marrow**. Not a brawl first but a reckoning: triage versus stewardship, mercy as hoarding versus mercy as sharing. Thane is sincere, tragic, *wrong and grieving* — never cruel.
3. **A4-03 — Marrow's reckoning.** Marrow's thread resolves: if hope was kept and the gap was crossed, Marrow turns from Thane and is gently reclaimed (**MARROW_REDEEMED**); otherwise Marrow makes a final, sorrowful stand. **Possible boss: Marrow and/or an Ascendancy guardian-construct.**
4. **A4-04 — The Warden wakes.** The Warden answers Wren's Song and offers the choice. It reveals the truth to the depth the party earned — that the Skyborn *departed*, the promise they left, and what the Wellspring truly is. (Fuller if the Skyborn truth is whole.)
5. **A4-05 — Thane's last grief.** Thane's certainty is laid bare as fear for the people he loves. If enough of the world was united behind a kinder answer, he can be persuaded to stand down (**THANE_PERSUADED**).
6. **A4-06 — The final choice.** At the heart of the world Wren is offered the path the whole journey has gated open:
   - **Share** the aether and remake how the sky lives together → **Ending A "The Shared Dawn"** *(offered only if the factions are united)*
   - Let the Wellspring **sleep** forever → **Ending B "The Long Quiet"**
   - **Take** the power for the chosen few → **Ending C "The Ascendant Throne"**
   - **Wake** the Warden as a partner and go to find the vanished Skyborn → **Ending D "The Wandering Star"** *(secret; offered only if the deepest truth was earned)*
   - **A4-06b — Bramble steps forward.** Once Wren commits the choice, the moment lands through **Bramble**. On the **Share / Sleep / Take** paths, the Song needs one more true thing to complete or quiet it, and Bramble — the tool that became a person — gives the last of itself freely ("I am not afraid"), the emotional fulcrum of the finale. On the secret **Wake** path it is the opposite: Bramble is made **whole** and travels on, the reward for the deepest road. *The world-choice is felt through the one who loved the world most.*
7. **A4-07 — Resolution.** The chosen ending plays out and the Song resolves (or quiets) accordingly. *(Full endings and exact unlock conditions in `04_BRANCHES_ENDINGS.md`.)*

**Boss / encounter highlights:** Marrow / Ascendancy guardian-construct (A4-03) · the Warden as the final *choice*, not a fight.

**Emotional turn:** The realization at the center of the brief — that saving a world is not one magic fix but a decision about how everyone lives together — placed entirely in the hands of one small, brave keeper.
