# Key Scenes — Dialogue & Cutscene Script — "Aetherbound" (working title)

> Built on `00_BRIEF.md` (anchor + Canon rulings), `01_WORLD.md` (world & terms), `02_CHARACTERS.md` (voices), `03_MAIN_STORY.md` (beats & ledger), and `04_BRANCHES_ENDINGS.md` (branches & endings). Every scene is tagged with its canonical **beat ID** so it maps to the Beat Ledger. Lines are written for a 10-year-old reading at a 6th-grade level: short, vivid, gentle. No profanity, no gore. Loss is shown as light, not blood.

---

## How to read this script

- **Scene heading:** `[BEAT ID] — TITLE — LOCATION`
- **`[Bracketed]`** text is cutscene / stage direction, camera, art, and music cues.
- **CHARACTER NAME:** dialogue. Lines kept short and distinct per the voice guide.
- **Branch forks** are labeled with the branch ID (BR2, BR3) and the flag each fork sets.
- **`(parenthetical)`** beside a name is a quick acting note.
- **The Song motif** = the central melody from `01_WORLD.md` §8 / `02_CHARACTERS.md` casting notes. "Whole and golden" = safe; "thin/faltering" = Fading; "fragments" = Bramble; "silence and static" = Marrow.

Voice quick-key (match exactly):
**Wren** earnest, listening, asks more than declares · **Sable** dry, fast, deflecting · **Tam** excitable, tumbling, course-correcting · **Mira** gentle, precise, measured · **Kestrel** formal, clipped, military · **Bramble** fragmented-poetic, images and gaps · **Thane** measured, sorrowful, reasonable · **Marrow** hollow, hushed, aching · **Warden** calm, ancient, questioning.

# 1. A1-01 — THE LAMP-BELL — MEADOWMOOR (COLD OPEN)

> *Player's first impression. Establish Wren, Keeper Pell, the world, and the Song. Make it shine.*

```
[BLACK SCREEN. One clear chime — a single child-voice note, unaccompanied: the Song motif, just the first three notes. Hold it.]

[FADE IN: a sky of soft morning gold. Clouds drift slow. The CAMERA falls gently — past a drifting island, past sheep on long tethers grazing upside-down hillsides — and settles on MEADOWMOOR: windmills turning, rooftop gardens, one tall stone tower at the island's heart. Everything moves a little, like a boat at anchor. It is beautiful and small and quiet.]

[SOUND: a deep, warm BELL. Once. Twice. The lamp-bell. Doors open. People stretch in doorways and look toward the tower.]
```

[TEXT, low and unhurried, centered:]
*"The lamp is lit. We're aloft. All's well."*
*— what every island says to its children*

```
[INTERIOR — THE LAMP TOWER. Warm, cluttered, beloved. Tools on hooks. A cracked window. At the center, a great-lamp: a wellstone the size of a melon in an iron cradle. Its light is GOLD — but thin gold, with a creeping rim of anxious blue at the edges. It flickers like a held breath.]

[WREN kneels at the cradle, both hands cupped near the stone — not touching, listening. Her throat-stone on its cord glows faint. She is small in a coat two sizes too big. KEEPER PELL, old and steady, stands behind her with a tuning-cloth over one shoulder.]
```

KEEPER PELL: (gentle) Morning bell's rung, Wren. The island's awake.

KEEPER PELL: But our lamp isn't, quite. Tell me what you hear.

WREN: (a beat late — she was really listening) It's... tired.

WREN: Like a song someone's humming in their sleep.

WREN: It keeps almost stopping. Then it remembers how to go.

```
[PELL crouches beside her, knees creaking. He does not look surprised. He has known about her gift a long time.]
```

KEEPER PELL: Most folk just see a light. You hear the whole heart of it.

KEEPER PELL: That's a rare ear, child. Rare and quiet. We keep it quiet.

WREN: (small) I know.

```
[>>> TUTORIAL PROMPT: LISTEN. The screen softens; a faint ring spreads from Wren. Aether "shows" — golden threads in the lamp, a thin pulse running down into the floor, toward the heart of the island.]
```

KEEPER PELL: Go on. Listen — the way I taught you. Hands soft. Don't grab. Just... ask.

```
[Wren closes her eyes. The SONG MOTIF rises — golden but frayed at the edges, a note dropping out here and there. The camera follows the threads of light DOWN through the tower floor, down through the island's roots, toward something deep and distant. For one heartbeat the music almost connects to something vast below — then loses it. Wren gasps softly and opens her eyes.]
```

WREN: (quiet wonder) It goes down. Way down. Past the island.

WREN: There's something at the bottom of everything, Keeper. And the song... comes from there.

KEEPER PELL: (carefully, not denying) Aye. The old folk say all the lamps in the sky drink from one root.

KEEPER PELL: They say a lot of things. (beat) Some of them true.

```
[Pell rises. He lays a hand on the cradle, then gives the great-lamp a polishing wipe with his cloth — a tender, practiced motion. The gold steadies a little under his care.]
```

KEEPER PELL: Here's all you need to know this morning. The light isn't ours to use up.

KEEPER PELL: It's ours to *tend*. A lamp is a garden, not a fire. You don't burn a garden to keep warm.

WREN: (repeating it like a lesson loved) "You don't fix a garden by burning the rest of it."

KEEPER PELL: (smiles) You've been listening to more than the stone.

```
[A small alarm-chime from outside. The light through the cracked window dims — a SHADOW passes over Meadowmoor. Something big is moving in the sky. Pell's smile fades. He moves to the window.]

[Through the glass: far off, a dark, square-sailed shape against the gold — an Ascendancy harvester-ship, slate-grey, all iron and order, sliding toward the island. The MUSIC drops the Song motif entirely. A cold, grand, mournful brass theme threads in underneath — Thane's empire, heard before it's named.]
```

KEEPER PELL: (low, to himself) ...Already?

WREN: (rising, throat-stone brightening with fear) Keeper? Who is that?

KEEPER PELL: (turning, gentle but grave) Trouble that flies a flag, child.

KEEPER PELL: Stay close to the lamp. Whatever happens today — (he stops, chooses the kind words) — you remember it was lit, and you were here, and that mattered.

```
[He puts the tuning-cloth into her hands. The CAMERA pulls back through the window, up and out, until tiny Meadowmoor and its one gold lamp sit small under the shadow of the great grey ship.]

[TITLE CARD, simple, over the drifting sky:]

                         A E T H E R B O U N D

[Hold. The first three notes of the Song chime once more — then cut to gameplay.]
```

# 2. A1-04 — THE SONG AWAKENS — MEADOWMOOR

> *Wren is publicly revealed as a Resonant. The hinge of Act I.*

```
[EXTERIOR — THE TOWER SQUARE. Villagers crowd back against the walls. The ASCENDANCY OFFICER stands at the lamp cradle — polite, joyless, a clipboard of orders in gloved hands. Two Tinplate automatons flank him, blank-faced. KEEPER PELL stands between the officer and the lamp. WREN, TAM, and SABLE are at the square's edge near the docks.]

[The great-lamp is failing — the gold guttering hard now, blue swallowing it, because the harvester's pull has already begun siphoning the island's charge. The light strobes. People murmur, frightened.]
```

ASCENDANCY OFFICER: (flat, reading) "By order of the High Regent, this island's great-stone is requisitioned for the greater good."

ASCENDANCY OFFICER: It is failing regardless. We are simply... collecting it sooner.

KEEPER PELL: You're pulling it dry from your deck. That's not collecting. That's robbing a dying man's last breath.

ASCENDANCY OFFICER: (no heat) I take no joy in it, keeper. That changes nothing.

```
[The lamp SHUDDERS. The gold drops to a pinpoint — then GOES OUT. Darkness slaps across the square. People cry out. The MUSIC stops dead — total silence, the scariest sound in the game.]

[A cold wind. At the dimming rim of the island, faint Hollow wisps stir in the new dark, drifting toward the square, toward warmth.]
```

TAM: (grabbing Wren's sleeve, whispering) Wren — the lamp's *out.* It's never been out.

```
[Wren stares at the dead cradle. Everyone she loves is in the dark. The Hollow are coming. Her throat-stone flares bright with fear — then something changes in her face. She stops being afraid of being seen. She steps forward.]
```

WREN: (under her breath, to herself) ...It's not gone. I can still hear it. It's just — quiet.

SABLE: (sharp, reaching for her) Kid — *don't* —

```
[Too late. Wren crosses to the cradle, kneels, and cups her hands around the dead stone. She doesn't grab. She asks. She HUMS — the Song motif, clear and small and child-bright, the first time we hear her voice carry it.]

[>>> TUTORIAL PROMPT: STEADY. A ring of warm gold spreads from her hands. The MUSIC blooms back — the Song, whole and golden, pouring out of one small girl in a dark square.]

[The wellstone catches. Light climbs back up it — gold, real gold, brighter than it's been in years. The Hollow wisps falter at the rim and drift back from the light. The whole square is bathed in warm gold, and at its center kneels Wren, glowing, astonished at her own hands.]

[Silence. Every villager stares. Pell closes his eyes — half pride, half dread. The Officer slowly lowers his clipboard.]
```

ASCENDANCY OFFICER: (quiet, almost reverent — and that's the threat) ...A Resonant.

ASCENDANCY OFFICER: A natural one. Here. On a sheep-island.

```
[He looks at her not with anger but with appetite — the cold interest of someone who has just found something valuable. The Tinplate automatons turn their blank heads toward Wren in unison.]
```

WREN: (still kneeling, the light fading to a steady gold, frightened of what she just did) I — I just wanted to keep it lit.

KEEPER PELL: (stepping between her and the officer, voice low and final) She's a child.

ASCENDANCY OFFICER: She is the most important person on this island, keeper. (beat) And she is coming with us.

```
[The MUSIC tightens — Thane's grand cold theme over Wren's small motif, the two pressed against each other. Hold on Wren's face: the moment her small life ends and a much larger, more frightening one begins. CUT to the conscription beat.]
```

# 3. A1-06 — LEAVING HOME — MEADOWMOOR DOCKS → THE SKY-ROADS

> *The first goodbye. Pell gives the Keeper's Lamp. Sets `SABLE_RECRUITED`, `TAM_RECRUITED`, `HAS_KEEPERS_LAMP`.*

```
[EXTERIOR — THE MEADOWMOOR DOCKS. A narrow landing-arm jutting off the island's edge into open sky. SABLE's ship, the DRIFTWING, is half-loaded with salvage, keel-stone glowing, wings half-unfurled. KEEPER PELL has bought them seconds by re-lighting a second lamp across the square to draw the officer off. Wren, Tam, and Sable hurry up the gangplank. Pell stands at the dock's foot — staying behind.]

[MUSIC: Sable's wry sea-shanty swing, but slowed, with the tender minor refrain underneath. This is a goodbye.]
```

SABLE: (at the wheel, fast) Lines off, gauges green — kid, robot-fan, *aboard,* we are not waiting for the parade.

TAM: (scrambling up, arms full of his ward-gadget and a sack) Aboard! Mostly aboard! My elbow's aboard —

WREN: (frozen at the top of the gangplank, looking back) Keeper — you're not coming.

```
[Pell climbs halfway up the gangplank. He's calm. He's already decided. He takes Wren's hands.]
```

KEEPER PELL: Somebody has to keep the lamp, Wren. That's the whole job. You taught the lamp; the lamp didn't teach you.

WREN: (voice cracking) Then I'll stay and help you tend it. I'm the keeper too. I can —

KEEPER PELL: (gently, firmly) No. Listen to me. One last lesson.

KEEPER PELL: A keeper doesn't tend one lamp. A keeper tends *the light.* And the light's biggest now where *you're* going. Down to the root of it.

```
[He reaches into his coat and lifts out a wellstone unlike the others — small, smooth, and glowing a deep steady gold that does not flicker. He presses it into Wren's hands and folds her fingers over it.]
```

KEEPER PELL: This is the Keeper's Lamp. Oldest stone on the island. It's never once gone grey.

KEEPER PELL: (smiling through it) When you're scared — and you'll be scared — you hold this and you remember: somebody, somewhere, is always keeping a light for you.

WREN: (clutching it, the throat-stone flaring) I don't know if I'm brave enough for this.

KEEPER PELL: Brave isn't a thing you *are*, child. It's a thing you do, scared, one more time.

```
[A shout from the square — the Officer has noticed. Tinplate footsteps clang closer. SABLE guns the keel; the Driftwing strains at its lines.]
```

SABLE: (over her shoulder, gentle for once) Old man. She'll be safe with me. (beat, the truth slipping out) ...I mean that.

KEEPER PELL: (to Sable, a nod) I know you do. (to Wren) Go. Go now. Keep it lit.

```
[Pell steps back down the gangplank and cuts the last mooring line himself. The Driftwing leaps free of the dock into open gold sky. Wren rushes to the rail.]
```

WREN: (shouting, wind taking it) KEEPER —!

KEEPER PELL: (small now, on the dock, hand raised — not waving goodbye, but holding up his lamp-lighter, a keeper's salute) *The lamp is lit. We're aloft. All's well!*

```
[CAMERA pulls up and away. Meadowmoor shrinks below — one small green island, one tall tower, one old man holding up a light, getting smaller and smaller against the vast bright sky. Wren stands at the rail clutching the Keeper's Lamp to her chest, both hands cupped around it like a flame, exactly the silhouette from the cold open.]

[The throat-stone slowly dims from fear-bright to a soft glow as Tam comes and stands beside her, not saying anything, just there.]
```

TAM: (quietly, for once not tumbling) He's gonna be okay, Wren.

WREN: (not looking away from the shrinking island) ...You don't know that.

TAM: (beat) No. But I'm gonna say it anyway. That's what you do.

```
[The MUSIC lifts — Wren's small motif, now with one more instrument under it: she is not alone. The Driftwing banks onto a glowing wind-road and sails into the open sky. FADE.]
```

# 3b. A1-06b — THE KEEPER'S HABIT — THE SKY-ROADS (FIRST NIGHT)

> *A small, quiet beat that makes Pell a thread, not a memory. Plants his ritual + saying as the things the finale will pay off. Sets `PELL_RITUAL_TAUGHT`.*

```
[NIGHT on the open Sky-Roads. The Driftwing rides a dark wind-road between two faraway island-lights. Below decks, the keel-stone in its iron cradle runs nervous — its gold thin, stuttering, the same anxious blue Wren knows from home. SABLE dozes at the wheel above. TAM is asleep in a coil of rope, gauntlet still ticking. WREN sits alone by the cradle, the Keeper's Lamp in her lap, unable to sleep.]
```

WREN: (to the nervous keel-stone, very soft) ...You too, huh. Long way from anywhere.

```
[She sets the Keeper's Lamp down beside the cradle. Then she does something practiced, something her hands know without asking: two gentle taps on the iron cradle with two fingers. A pause. One slow breath.]
```

WREN: (under her breath, the way you'd say it to a child) The lamp is lit. We're aloft. All's well.

```
[She does NOT use the Song — no glow, no ring. Just the taps, the breath, the words. It's a keeper's habit, plain hand-craft. And the keel-stone, soothed by a steady hand near it, settles a half-shade warmer. The stutter eases. It's small. It's enough.]

[She looks at her own two fingers, then at the Keeper's Lamp, and her throat tightens — because the habit is his, and doing it without him is the first time the loss is real.]
```

WREN: (whisper, to the Lamp) That's how you do it, Keeper. Two taps. A breath. Same as always.

WREN: (a watery almost-laugh) You always said the lamp doesn't need a big speech. It just needs to know somebody's still there.

```
[BRAMBLE, newly awake — wait, no: Bramble isn't aboard yet. TAM stirs instead, half-asleep, and shuffles over to sit beside her, not quite awake enough to make a joke.]
```

TAM: (mumbling, sleepy) ...you did the tap thing. The Pell thing.

WREN: (quiet) It's mine now. (beat) I guess that's what he gave me. Not the Lamp. The — the how.

TAM: (settling his head back, eyes closing) S'a good how. (beat, fading out) ...he's gonna be okay, Wren.

WREN: (looking out at the dark wind-road, holding the Lamp to her chest — the cold-open silhouette) ...That's what you do. Say it anyway.

```
[The keel-stone holds its steadier glow. Wren keeps her hand near the cradle, keeping the light, all the way into the dark. The Song motif plays once, soft and unaccompanied — but calm now, not afraid. FADE.]

[>>> FLAG SET: PELL_RITUAL_TAUGHT = true.]
```

# 4. A1-10 — AWAKENING BRAMBLE — THE WRECK BENEATH HOLLOWGATE

> *Bramble's first fragmented words; the Song motif made manifest. Sets `BRAMBLE_RECRUITED`, `TUT_SHAPE`.*

```
[INTERIOR — DEEP IN THE SKYBORN WRECK. A vast ribbed hull, like the inside of a sleeping whale made of brass and pale stone. Dead aether-veins run along the walls — grey, lightless. The party's lamps are the only warmth. TAM is giddy, touching everything. SABLE keeps one hand near her spyglass. WREN walks slowly — she keeps stopping, head tilted.]
```

TAM: (whispering at full volume) Wren — *Wren* — this whole place is one machine. A ship that *thinks.* Do you know what that's worth? Don't answer, you don't, it's worth EVERYTHING —

SABLE: (dry) Keep your voice down, Brightgear. Old wrecks are like old dogs. You let 'em sleep.

WREN: (stopping dead) ...Sable. Quiet for a second.

```
[Wren closes her eyes. The Song motif rises — but here it's almost gone, a single faint note far off, like someone humming three rooms away. She follows it. The CAMERA tracks her to a low alcove. There, curled small among the dead veins, is BRAMBLE — a round, moss-grown brass construct the size of a big cat, dark and still. Its chest-grille is black. But behind it, deep down, a single ember of gold pulses. Once. Slowly.]
```

WREN: (kneeling, hushed) Oh. Oh, you poor thing. You're *singing.*

TAM: (creeping up) That's — that's a tender-construct. Skyborn-make. It's not even a weapon, look at it, it's *gentle* — is it broken?

WREN: (shaking her head slowly) No. Asleep. Like the lamp was. Just... almost out.

SABLE: (warning) Kid. Whatever you're about to do —

WREN: (already deciding) It's hurting, Sable. I can hear it hurting. I have to.

```
[>>> TUTORIAL PROMPT: SHAPE. This is bigger than Steady. Wren places both hands on Bramble's cold chest-grille. The screen dims to her and the construct alone. She doesn't just hum — she *gives.* A thread of gold flows from her throat-stone, down her arms, into the dark ember. Her own light dims as Bramble's brightens. (Foreshadow of cost — Songsickness.)]

[The Song motif builds — and for the first time we hear it answered. Bramble's chest-stone catches the tune and plays it BACK, in broken music-box fragments, notes missing, but unmistakably the same melody. Light climbs through the moss-grown joints. The optic-eyes flutter, then glow, and tilt — up, at Wren.]
```

BRAMBLE: (first words, soft and broken, like a radio finding a station) ...warm. The warm came back. I lost the — the word for warm. You brought it.

WREN: (tears in her eyes, smiling) Hello. Can you hear me?

BRAMBLE: (head tilting, studying her) I held a tower once. Or the tower held me. The word for both is gone.

BRAMBLE: (a fragment surfacing) There were... so many of us. Singing. Then the singing went... *away.* Not died. *Away.* (it seems puzzled by its own memory)

TAM: (awed, very quiet) ...It remembers the Skyborn.

BRAMBLE: (turning its lamp-eyes on each of them, slow) You are small. (to Wren especially) The makers were small, at the start. Small is where light begins.

WREN: (gently) What's your name?

BRAMBLE: (a long pause — searching, failing) ...gone. That word too. (looks down at the moss grown into its own brass arms, almost wondering) Green. Growing. In me.

WREN: (soft) Bramble. The green things that grow back, even in the dark. Can I call you Bramble?

BRAMBLE: (the chest-stone pulses, pleased — a fragment of the Song plays, one note more complete than before) ...Bramble. Yes. I will keep that one. I will not lose that one.

```
[Wren sways — Songsickness, the first taste. Her vision greys for a second; she steadies on Tam's shoulder. The throat-stone is dim now from what she gave.]
```

SABLE: (catching her, gruff and worried) Easy. Easy, kid. You spent yourself.

WREN: (woozy, but smiling at Bramble) Worth it.

BRAMBLE: (looking at her with something like recognition across centuries) You hear it. The Song. Truly hear it. (a beat, almost mournful) The last one who heard me like that... I cannot find their face. But I know the shape of being *heard.* Thank you.

```
[A low GRINDING from deep in the wreck — the great crane-husk waking, disturbed. Dust sifts down. Everyone looks up. The MUSIC tilts toward danger.]
```

SABLE: (instantly captain again) And *that* would be the old dog waking up. New friend, can you walk?

BRAMBLE: (rising on small unsteady legs, moss shedding) I can follow the warm one. (it steps to Wren's side and stays there) I choose to.

```
[Wren and Bramble, side by side — the warm girl and the little brass child, both lit by the same gold, the Song motif and its broken echo braided together. Then they run for the exit as the boss stirs. CUT to A1-11, the Sleepless Crane.]
```

```
[EXTERIOR — THE CINDERWORKS. A grim industrial skyland: furnace-stacks, soot-clouds, marching order. Aether-pumps the size of houses drain charge from captured smaller islands lashed alongside. In the middle distance, on raised gantries, conscripted Resonants in cracked augment-rigs sway at their stations — Songsick, grey, hollowing. The party watches from cover, sick at the sight. KESTREL: (a flicker — then iron) My wants are not the objective. (beat) Choose. Come quietly, or resist. Either way I do my duty.

```

## A2-08 — FORK A: "Show her the truth" *(sets `KESTREL_DOUBT`)*

```
[Wren does not run. She steps PAST Sable, toward Kestrel — small, unarmed, hands open. The party tenses. Kestrel's grip shifts on her blade, confused by a target that walks closer.]
```

WREN: (gently) Okay. I'll come. But first — you have to look. Just look. One minute.

WREN: (pointing, not afraid) Up there. The Resonants on the rigs. The ones like me.

```
[She lifts her hand and quietly Listens — a soft ring spreads, and for a moment the SOUND of the gantry comes through to the player and to Kestrel: not machinery, but voices — the thin, broken Song of the conscripts, each one a melody with notes torn out. It's the saddest sound in the game. Kestrel goes very still.]
```

KESTREL: (low) ...What is that sound.

WREN: It's them. That's their Song. The rigs are supposed to make it *louder.* (her voice shakes) They're tearing it apart instead. That's not making more light. That's burning the garden.

KESTREL: (a soldier's denial) They are volunteers. Honored servants of the Ascendancy. I escort their transfers myself, I have *seen* —

WREN: (quiet, terrible) Then really see. Listen with me.

```
[Camera holds on one conscript on the gantry — gaunt, grey, swaying. The Song-thread coming off them flickers and... drops a note. Then another. The light in their rig stutters toward grey. They sag. Two Tinplates move to prop them upright and reset the rig, mechanical, uncaring. Kestrel watches a person be treated as a failing part.]

[MUSIC: Kestrel's strict martial cadence cracks — a single warm falcon-russet note bleeds through the iron, then is smothered.]
```

KESTREL: (almost to herself) "Order is mercy." That's what we say. That's what I — (she stops)

KESTREL: (turning on Wren, but the anger is fear) Why would you show me this. I have a duty. You have made it *harder.*

WREN: (the brave-scared thing Pell taught her) Because I think you're the kind of person who'd rather it be hard and true than easy and a lie.

KESTREL: (long silence; she looks at the gantry, then at her own gloved hand) ...I followed orders because order is mercy. (quiet) I begin to fear order without conscience is just a tidier cruelty.

KESTREL: (sheathing her blade an inch — not surrender, but doubt) Go. Quickly, before I remember my orders. We are not finished, Resonant. But I — (she struggles) — I require time to think.

```
[>>> FLAG SET: KESTREL_DOUBT = true. She lets them pass. Hold on Kestrel alone on the gantry, staring up at the conscripts, her certainty cracked. The single russet note plays once more, unsmothered now. CUT to A2-09 (Marrow), shared.]
```

## A2-08 — FORK B: "Resist & evade" *(sets `KESTREL_PURSUER`)*

```
[Sable yanks Wren back. Tam hurls a Bottle-Bolt that bursts in a flash of light and smoke across the gantry.]
```

TAM: (already running) Okay okay okay — TACTICAL retreat! Which is just running but with a cooler name! GO!

SABLE: (sprinting, herding Wren) Up the crane-arm, kid, MOVE — Bramble, light!

```
[A chase across the Cinderworks gantries — gameplay sequence: dodging steam-vents, leaping gaps, Kestrel pursuing with short glider-bursts, relentless and precise. She never panics, never gives up, calls out clipped commands to Tinplate units. The party out-flies her by inches and tumbles aboard the Driftwing as it pulls away into the soot-clouds.]

[FINAL SHOT of the fork: Kestrel skids to a stop at the gantry's edge, wings spread, watching the ship escape. She does not rage. She simply notes it. She lifts her oath-token, looks at it, and closes her fist.]
```

KESTREL: (to herself, cold and steady) Noted. They are fast. They are loyal to the child. (beat) That is a thread I can follow.
