# Side Quests — "Aetherbound" (working title)

> Built on `00_BRIEF.md` (anchor + Canon rulings) and `01_WORLD.md`. Every quest here ties to the game's themes — **stewardship vs. exploitation, found family, the courage of small people, and hope** — and is scoped to be short and buildable, not sprawling. Loss is handled gently (fading to light, "the Long Quiet"); villains and strangers are sincere; no gore. All names are original.

---

## How to read this doc

- **Acts** match the brief: I (Keeper's Lamp), II (Sinking Roads), III (Fading World), IV (Wellspring).
- **Skylands** are the canon eight: Meadowmoor, Hollowgate, Glasswastes, Verdance, Cinderworks, Thornholt, Stillmere, The Hush.
- The **Song** verbs — *Listen, Steady, Shape* — and the firm aether rules (no free energy; overreach causes Songsickness) are reused exactly as in `01_WORLD.md`.
- Quest IDs are a **contract for the engine/data**: `CQ-<NAME>` for companion quests, `SQ-##` for world/town quests, `MA-##` for mastery/collection arcs. See the master table at the end.

# 1. Companion Quests (one per party member)

Each companion quest opens when that character has been in the party a while and the world is ready for their next emotional step. Each grants a **signature ability** or **signature gear** that mechanically expresses the character's growth.

---

## CQ-WREN — "The Listening Stone"
- **Member:** Wren (apprentice aether-tender; hidden Resonant; heart of the story).
- **Unlocks:** Act III, returning to **Meadowmoor** after the Fading.
- **Hook:** A floated lantern arrives on the wind from Meadowmoor with no message — only a strip of cloth from Wren's old master's coat, tied to the handle the careful way Pell tied everything. Wren needs to go home. She finds the village dimmed and emptied — evacuated to The Hush when the lamp finally failed — and the keeper's house standing open, his keeping-journal still on the bench.
- **Steps:**
  1. Sail to a half-dimmed, abandoned Meadowmoor; a few old folk who would not leave are tending one another by candlelight.
  2. **Listen** at the dead village lamp — Wren hears no Song at all, and panics that her own gift is failing.
  3. Find her master's keeping-journal in the lamp-house; learn that, with the stone too far gone to relight, Pell got every soul out the plain keeper's way — driving off and out-waiting the gathering Hollow and stretching the dying stone's last charge by candle, cloth, and patience (no Song; Pell was never a Resonant) — before he walked to the boats with the last of them.
  4. A drift of gentle Hollow gathers at the cold lamp, drawn to the last warmth — the few who stayed fear them.
  5. Wren must choose how to face them (the choice below) rather than fight.
- **Continuity:** This quest pairs with the main-story Pell beats (**A3-02b/A3-02c**, his reunion and loss at The Hush). It is Wren's place to grieve the *home* and the man — and it reads gently whether the player visits before or after Pell's passing.
- **Choice / twist:** The twist is that the lamp **cannot** be saved — there is not enough aether left, and her master knew it. Wren chooses to **re-tune and calm the Hollow** (the kind, hard answer from the world bible) and lead them out to the dimming edge, OR to **Steady** the lamp for one last night so the few who stayed can say goodbye to their home by its light before they leave. Either way she accepts that stewardship sometimes means letting go gracefully, not "one magic fix."
- **Reward:**
  - *Story:* Wren stops fearing she must save everything alone; the journal's last line ("a keeper keeps people, not lamps") reframes her arc.
  - *Gameplay:* Signature ability **Keeper's Calm** — a party-wide Listen that reveals every Hollow and wellstone on a map and lets Wren pacify weak Hollow without combat. Gear: **Master's Tuning Fork** (boosts Steady duration).
- **Theme payoff:** Courage of the small and true stewardship — the lamp-keeper learns the world is a garden, not a machine you can fix by force.

## CQ-SABLE — "The Island That Isn't There"
- **Member:** Captain Sable Vance (wry salvager who lost her home island; cynic → believer).
- **Unlocks:** Act II, once the party has free run of Sable's ship between **Hollowgate** and **Thornholt**.
- **Hook:** Sable keeps charting a wind-road to an island that no longer exists — her drowned home, **Larkfall**. A salvager at Thornholt claims he pulled a still-glowing keel-stone from "a wreck near where Larkfall used to be" and will sell it… for a price that smells like a trap.
- **Steps:**
  1. Track the salvager through Thornholt's ramps and junk-markets; he wants Sable to fly a dangerous low run near the mist to "confirm the wreck."
  2. Make the low run; the keel-stone really is from Larkfall — and so is a single survivor's lock-box of letters.
  3. The letters reveal Sable's family didn't all sink: some were evacuated by a passing crew she always resented for "leaving too soon."
  4. The salvager reappears wanting the keel-stone for the black market; he isn't cruel, just desperate and broke.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — Sable wasn't abandoned; she was *saved* by people she's been bitter at for years. Choice: **give the salvager the keel-stone** (he can keep his own sinking crew aloft another season — stewardship/mercy), or **keep it** to upgrade Sable's ship. Generosity here unlocks the salvager as a recurring discount vendor; keeping it gives the immediate ship buff.
- **Reward:**
  - *Story:* Sable stops flying *away* from her grief and starts flying *toward* people. Her cynicism cracks into belief that the sky is worth saving, not just out-flying.
  - *Gameplay:* Ship upgrade **Larkfall Keel** (longer Sky-Road range, can do one safe low-mist run per region to reach hidden quests). Signature captain ability **All Hands** (party-wide revive-once buff before a hard fight).
- **Theme payoff:** Found family — the family Sable mourned became the strangers who carried her, and she chooses to pass that mercy on.

## CQ-TAM — "One Good Spark"
- **Member:** Tam Brightgear (young inventor; reckless tinkerer → responsible).
- **Unlocks:** Act II, at **Hollowgate**'s salvagers' guild after the first relic dungeon.
- **Hook:** Tam reverse-engineers a Skyborn relic into a "charge-doubler" and wants to demo it at Hollowgate's great-lamp to prove small inventors matter. Wren can **Listen** and tell something about the device is *wrong* — it doesn't make aether, it just spends it faster and louder.
- **Steps:**
  1. Help Tam gather three relic parts across Hollowgate's undermarket (light fetch + a Hollow scuffle in the old wreck below the city).
  2. Assemble the doubler; the test flares bright and the crowd cheers.
  3. The great-lamp begins to **gutter** — the doubler is draining it, exactly the Ascendancy's mistake in miniature (no free energy, ever).
  4. Hollow stir toward the destabilizing lamp; the party must hold them off while Tam decides.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — Tam's "miracle" is the harvest-everything philosophy in a small box. Choice: **let Tam smash his own invention** (he grows up, chooses responsibility over glory) or **convince him to repurpose it** into a *Steady*-amplifier that helps Wren stretch charge instead of burning it. Both are growth; the second requires a prior Wren bonding scene.
- **Reward:**
  - *Story:* Tam stops chasing the big flashy spark and learns the courage to build small, honest things.
  - *Gameplay:* Gadget **Brightgear Stabilizer** (a deployable that shields the party from Hollow "drain" attacks) OR, on the repurpose path, **Resonant Coil** (lets Tam extend Wren's Steady to the whole party in a pinch).
- **Theme payoff:** Stewardship vs. exploitation, courage of the small — the kid genius rejects the exact shortcut the empire chose.

## CQ-MIRA — "The Bell That Lied"
- **Member:** Mira (young Lantern Order healer; faith vs. evidence → holds both).
- **Unlocks:** Act II–III, on or after the first visit to **Stillmere**.
- **Hook:** Mira is sent to ring the relighting bell at a small Order chapel on a sinking edge-island; the elders promise the bell "calls the makers' light." Mira has started to doubt — sick people keep coming and the lamps keep dimming anyway.
- **Steps:**
  1. Travel to the failing chapel-island; tend three ailing villagers with real medicine and real comfort.
  2. Discover the chapel's "miracle lamp" is secretly topped each night from a hidden wellstone by a kind old sister — the "miracle" is quiet human care, dressed as faith.
  3. The old sister begs Mira not to tell — the lie keeps the villagers hopeful enough to evacuate calmly.
  4. An Order overseer arrives to "verify the miracle" for a sermon.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — the faith isn't a fraud exactly; it's love wearing a costume. Choice: **tell the truth gently** (faith and evidence can coexist; the villagers grieve but trust Mira more) or **keep the kind lie** for now and quietly ensure the evacuation. This mirrors the brief's Stillmere expose-vs-protect branch at personal scale.
- **Reward:**
  - *Story:* Mira learns to hold both faith and evidence — to comfort *and* tell the truth — instead of choosing one.
  - *Gameplay:* Signature ability **Steady Hands** (a heal that also cures Songsickness/grey-vision status, the only such cleanse in the game). Gear: **Candle-Boat Censer** (small party regen in lit zones).
- **Theme payoff:** Hope grounded in honesty — that real care, not denial, is what keeps people lit.

## CQ-KESTREL — "The Names on the Wall"
- **Member:** Kestrel (defecting Ascendancy sky-knight; duty → conscience; redemption).
- **Unlocks:** Act III, after Kestrel is recruited (branch) and the party reaches **Cinderworks** territory post-Fading.
- **Hook:** Kestrel learns her old knight-cohort is being sent on a "mercy harvest" — draining a still-inhabited island because Ascendancy math says its people can't be saved anyway. Her former squire, a teenager named Finch, is among the crew following orders Kestrel once gave.
- **Steps:**
  1. Infiltrate a Cinderworks harvester-dock (stealth/persuade, not slaughter — these are sincere people serving a wrong cause).
  2. Find the muster wall where Kestrel's name was struck off as "deserter"; nearby, the names of the island's families to be displaced.
  3. Confront Finch, who believes Kestrel betrayed the code; debate duty vs. conscience.
  4. Reach the island ahead of the harvest and warn/evacuate its people with Sable's ship.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — Kestrel discovers the harvest order was signed in *her* old hand, from before she defected; she helped build this. Choice: **expose the order to Finch's whole cohort** (some defect with her — the harder, hopeful path) or **quietly stop this one harvest** and let Finch keep his post and his faith in the code. Exposing recruits an optional minor ally; the quiet path keeps Finch safe and loyal to Kestrel personally.
- **Reward:**
  - *Story:* Kestrel forgives herself enough to act — her redemption stops being about her past and becomes about who she protects now.
  - *Gameplay:* Signature ability **Knight's Vow** (a guard stance that intercepts hits aimed at weaker party members — Wren especially). Gear: **Reforged Crest** (her old Ascendancy sigil, melted and re-struck; raises party morale/resistance to fear effects).
- **Theme payoff:** The courage to change how we live together — a soldier of the hoarding empire chooses people over orders.

## CQ-BRAMBLE — "The Unfinished Song"
- **Member:** Bramble (small Skyborn construct; tool → person; the world's conscience).
- **Unlocks:** Act III, after Bramble has gained enough memory-fragments (ties into MA-01 below).
- **Hook:** Bramble's chest-stone keeps flickering an incomplete melody it cannot finish. It asks Wren, very simply, "Was I a person, or was I only made to be useful?" — and wants to find the maker who built it.
- **Steps:**
  1. Follow Bramble's memory-fragments to a quiet Skyborn workshop-relic on the edge of **Verdance**, gone green and overgrown.
  2. Inside, restore three dormant memory-glyphs (gentle Listen/Shape puzzles).
  3. The glyphs reveal Bramble was built not as a tool but as a *companion* for a Skyborn child who was lost in the Sundering — Bramble's "purpose" was simply to love and be loved.
  4. A final glyph offers Bramble the choice to wake fully (gaining selfhood) or stay safely simple.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — Bramble was never a tool; it was *family*, grieving a child for a thousand years without the words. Choice: Wren can **help Bramble complete the Song** (Bramble becomes fully a person, gains will and the ability to refuse orders — even the party's) or **let Bramble keep the Song unfinished** by its own wish, choosing gentleness over more grief. Completing the Song deepens Bramble into the whole, willing partner the secret Ending D is *felt through*, and lines up naturally with the deepest path — but it is **supporting flavor, not a hard gate**: Ending D's actual unlock is the `04` resolver's (`WARDEN_TRUTH_WHOLE AND ROOKWISE_RECRUITED AND MARROW_REDEEMED`).
- **Reward:**
  - *Story:* Bramble becomes the world's living conscience, able to speak the Skyborn's lost intent — pivotal lore for the endings, and the heart through which the Act IV choice lands (A4-06b).
  - *Gameplay:* Signature ability **Maker's Memory** (Bramble can play a fragment of the full Song to fully restore one party member or briefly relight a dead zone for safe passage). Gear: **Completed Heart-Stone** (raises Bramble's resonance; the in-fiction sign of a whole Bramble that accompanies Ending D — flavor that supports the path, not a gate; see `04`).
- **Theme payoff:** Found family and personhood — the "thing" was always a someone, and being made useful was never the point.

# 2. World / Town Side Quests (eight, across the canon skylands)

---

## SQ-01 — "The Last Inch" (sinking-island rescue)
- **Location:** A small unnamed edge-island near **Meadowmoor** / reachable from Hollowgate.
- **Act:** I–II.
- **Hook:** A shepherd family's island is down to its last inch above the mist; their tether-sheep won't cross to the rescue ship.
- **Steps:** (1) Reach the tilting island before nightfall. (2) Wren **Listens** to find the panicked sheep are spooked by Hollow wisps at the dimming edge. (3) Calm the wisps and herd the flock to Sable's ship. (4) Decide what to carry off in limited cargo space.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — the family wants to take their heavy great-lamp (their heritage); there's only room for *either* the lamp or the last few sheep (their livelihood). Choice teaches that you can't save everything — you help them choose what to carry into a new life.
- **Reward:** Reputation with sky-folk; a **tether-sheep mascot** for the ship (small passive luck/morale buff); supplies.
- **Theme tie-in:** Stewardship and the courage of small people — saving a world means rebuilding a life, not freezing it.

---

## SQ-02 — "Quiet the Quiet" (Hollow-calming problem)
- **Location:** **Hollowgate** undermarket / the old wreck beneath the city.
- **Act:** II.
- **Hook:** Merchants want a guild to *exterminate* the Hollow nesting in the wreck below the city; the noise and "drain" are scaring customers.
- **Steps:** (1) Descend into the cathedral-ship wreck. (2) Discover the Hollow are clustering around a cracked great-stone, drawn to its last warmth — they aren't attacking, just *hungry quiet*. (3) Wren can **re-tune** them or the guild can drive them off. (4) Stabilize or remove the cracked stone.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — driving the Hollow off just sends them up into the streets later; **calming/re-tuning** them is slower but actually solves it. Choice contrasts the easy violent fix vs. the patient kind one (echoing the whole game's thesis).
- **Reward:** Undermarket vendor discount; **Wellstone Shard** crafting material; if calmed, a tame "lamp-moth" Hollow follower that lights dark dungeons.
- **Theme tie-in:** Hope and stewardship — the kindest answer is the hardest and the only one that lasts.

---

## SQ-03 — "What the Mirror Kept" (Skyborn-relic mystery)
- **Location:** **The Glasswastes**.
- **Act:** II.
- **Hook:** A wandering scholar (optional tie to Rookwise) is convinced a buried Skyborn workshop holds a "record that explains the Sundering." Glass Hollow keep scattering his lantern-light and confusing his eyes.
- **Steps:** (1) Cross the ringing mirror-desert, using sound/Listen to navigate when sight fails. (2) Solve a light-redirection puzzle with mirror-shards to open the workshop. (3) Calm or evade the Glass Hollow that mistake reflections for prey. (4) Recover the Skyborn record-glyph.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — the "record" is incomplete and ambiguous: it can be read as proof the Skyborn *fled* (selfish) or *sacrificed themselves* (noble). Choice: hand the glyph to the scholar (who'll publish his hopeful interpretation) or to the Lantern Order (who'll bury it). This plants ambiguity that the endings resolve — never settle it here.
- **Reward:** Lore entry + a **memory-fragment for Bramble** (feeds MA-01 / CQ-BRAMBLE); **Mirror-Ward charm** (reduces enemy accuracy/confusion).
- **Theme tie-in:** The central mystery — every faction decides what the Skyborn "would have wanted" and builds their cause on a guess.

---

## SQ-04 — "Honest Salvage" (salvage / black-market dilemma)
- **Location:** **Thornholt**.
- **Act:** II–III.
- **Hook:** A rival salvage crew is selling "charged" wellstones that are actually near-dead grey stones painted gold — cheating desperate edge-islanders.
- **Steps:** (1) Catch the scam at a Thornholt junk-stall. (2) Trace it to a struggling crew whose own keel-stone is failing. (3) Wren can **Listen** to prove which stones are truly charged. (4) Confront the crew.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — the cheats are themselves about to sink and turned to fraud out of fear, not greed. Choice: **expose them publicly** (justice, but they lose their ship and join the displaced) or **help them go honest** by brokering a fair salvage contract (mercy + a recurring honest vendor). No villains here — just frightened people.
- **Reward:** Black-market access / discounts; **Salvager's Loupe** (reveals item quality and hidden loot); reputation.
- **Theme tie-in:** Stewardship vs. exploitation at street level — scarcity tempts good people to prey on each other, and there's a better way.

---

## SQ-05 — "Proof and Prayer" (faith-vs-evidence vignette)
- **Location:** Near **Stillmere**.
- **Act:** II–III.
- **Hook:** A pilgrim village outside the monastery is split: half want to evacuate (the lamp is clearly dying), half refuse, trusting the Order's promise that "the makers will relight it."
- **Steps:** (1) Speak with both sides; gather evidence (Listen-readings of the failing stone) and the Order's scripture. (2) A child is caught between, scared. (3) Bring evidence to the village elder and the Order sister. (4) Help broker the village's decision.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — the Order sister privately *knows* the lamp is failing but fears panic. Choice: **present hard evidence** (some leave safely, some faithful stay and must be helped later) or **frame the move as the makers' will** so the faithful will evacuate too (a kind half-truth). Foreshadows Stillmere's bigger secret and Mira's arc; both choices save lives differently.
- **Reward:** Lantern Order standing; **Pilgrim's Lantern** (light source that also slows Hollow approach); a follower NPC who later helps in Act III.
- **Theme tie-in:** Hope vs. denial — faith is precious, but it has to make room for the truth to actually save anyone.

## SQ-06 — "The Singing Door" (Glasswastes / puzzle)
- **Location:** **The Glasswastes** (deeper, a separate sealed vault from SQ-03).
- **Act:** II–III.
- **Hook:** A sealed Skyborn vault hums a chord; locals say it's never opened because "it only answers to a true voice." It's a Resonant lock — perfect for Wren.
- **Steps:** (1) Listen to learn the vault's three-note "key" chord. (2) Solve a resonance puzzle: match tones using ambient mirror-resonances and Bramble's fragment-melody. (3) Avoid overreach — pushing the Shape too hard risks Songsickness (a soft fail that costs a rest, not a death). (4) Open the door.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — inside is not treasure but a *dormant garden-construct* curled around a single living lamp-herb seed: the Skyborn left a seed, not a weapon. Choice: take the seed for Verdance restoration (feeds SQ-07 / MA-02) or wake the gentle construct as a guardian for a struggling town.
- **Reward:** **Wellstone (high-charge)**; if seed taken, unlocks a bonus at Verdance; a Song-puzzle tutorial that gates MA-01 mastery rewards.
- **Theme tie-in:** Stewardship — the makers' true legacy is a seed to grow, not a tool to spend.

## SQ-07 — "The Garden That Forgot to Stop" (Verdance overgrowth)
- **Location:** **Verdance**.
- **Act:** II–III.
- **Hook:** Untended aether has made the jungle grow violently; garden-constructs are "tidying" travelers (and a lost child) as if they were weeds.
- **Steps:** (1) Push through humming, oversized flora to find the child. (2) Discover the head garden-construct is following its last Skyborn order — "keep the garden perfect" — with no one left to update it. (3) Wren can **Shape** to ease the construct or Tam can try to rewire it. (4) Decide the garden's fate.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — the construct isn't malfunctioning; it's *grieving and obedient*, doing the only job it was ever given, forever. Choice: **gently retire it** (let it rest into the Long Quiet, mournful but peaceful) or **give it a new, kinder purpose** (tend the island *with* the people instead of pruning them out). Mirrors Bramble's tool-vs-person theme.
- **Reward:** **Lamp-herb harvest** (best healing items in the game); a Verdance vine-bridge shortcut opens; if reprogrammed, the construct becomes a passive resource-gatherer for the party's base.
- **Theme tie-in:** Stewardship vs. exploitation — wild growth and rigid order are both failures of care; balance is the answer.

## SQ-08 — "The Furnace and the Fed" (Cinderworks moral choice)
- **Location:** **The Cinderworks**.
- **Act:** III.
- **Hook:** An Ascendancy foreman runs an aether-pump draining a neighbor island, but the pump's stolen charge also keeps a workers' shelter — full of refugee families — warm and lit.
- **Steps:** (1) Infiltrate the works (these are sincere people, not monsters — see the workers' kids, the foreman's worry). (2) Learn the pump feeds *both* the harvest fleet *and* the shelter. (3) A conscripted Resonant near the pump is showing early Songsickness (canon: the Ascendancy's rigs burn Resonants out — this is what broke Marrow). (4) Decide how to act.
- **Choice / twist:** Twist — shutting the pump frees the neighbor island but cuts the refugees' heat; the foreman genuinely believes he's the only one keeping these families alive. Choice: **shut it down and evacuate the refugees** on Sable's ship (hard logistics, hopeful) or **sabotage only the harvest-feed** so the shelter keeps warmth but the fleet loses its haul (a clever middle path requiring Tam + Wren). Either way, **free the conscripted Resonant** before they hollow — a direct, gentle echo of Marrow's tragedy.
- **Choice / twist (continued):** No option is cost-free; the game shows that the empire's cruelty is *structural*, made of frightened decent people, not cartoon villainy.
- **Reward:** Frees a conscripted Resonant (optional minor ally / vendor); **Cinder-Forged gear** (heat-resistant equipment); evidence item usable in Kestrel's CQ and the main Stillmere/Ascendancy branches.
- **Theme tie-in:** Stewardship vs. exploitation, and that saving a world means *changing how we live together* — you can't just smash the machine, you have to care for who depended on it.

# 3. Collection / Mastery Arcs (two)

---

## MA-01 — "Song Fragments" (the music motif)
- **Theme tie-in:** Found family, hope, and the central mystery — reassembling the Skyborn's lost Song through Bramble and Wren.
- **Structure:**
  - **Seven Song Fragments** are scattered across the canon skylands — one each at Meadowmoor, Hollowgate, Glasswastes, Verdance, Cinderworks, Thornholt, and Stillmere. Each is found by Wren **Listening** at a resonant site (often as a small reward inside other quests, e.g. SQ-03, SQ-06, CQ-BRAMBLE) or by Bramble recognizing a fragment of its own memory.
  - As fragments are collected, **Bramble's chest-melody grows more complete**, and the world's background music gains a phrase — a diegetic, audible progress bar that the player *hears* getting closer to whole (per the World Bible's music north star).
  - The arc spans **Acts II–IV**; the final fragment is only reachable after **CQ-BRAMBLE**.
- **Rewards:**
  - **3 fragments:** **Resonant's Ear** — Listen range increased; reveals hidden fragment/quest sites on the map.
  - **5 fragments:** **Steadier Song** — Wren's Steady costs less and resists Songsickness longer.
  - **7 fragments (complete):** **The Whole Song** — a once-per-area ability to fully relight a dimmed zone for safe travel, and the full hopeful motif that **accompanies the secret Ending D** (in fiction, the whole Song is what wakes the Warden as a *partner* rather than a power source). This is **supporting flavor, not a hard gate** — Ending D's unlock is defined solely by `04`'s resolver (`WARDEN_TRUTH_WHOLE AND ROOKWISE_RECRUITED AND MARROW_REDEEMED`). Completing it also plays the game's full hopeful musical motif for the first time.
- **Buildable note:** Fragments are simple collectible flags; the audio "fill" is a layered music stem keyed to the count. No new systems required.

---

## MA-02 — "Relight the Lamps" (wellstone restoration)
- **Theme tie-in:** Stewardship — not hoarding aether, but tending and *sharing* what little remains so small communities stay aloft.
- **Structure:**
  - **Eight failing community lamps**, one per canon skyland (the Meadowmoor lamp is the tutorial/first; The Hush's is the hardest and last, only in Act III+). Wren can't *create* aether (firm rule), so the arc is about **rebalancing**: redirecting surplus charge from a wasteful source, repairing a leaking great-stone with Tam, or **Steadying** a stone to stretch its last charge while a town evacuates or adapts.
  - Each lamp is a short, self-contained task (a mini-puzzle or a small Hollow-calming), reusing existing zones — no new maps needed.
  - Progress is tracked on a **sky-map of lamps** that visibly turns from grey back toward gold as the player restores them — a visual echo of the music bar in MA-01.
- **Choice / twist:** A few lamps can only be relit by **taking charge from somewhere else** — the player must decide whom to brighten and whom to leave dim, teaching that stewardship is about fair sharing under scarcity, not infinite supply. Restoring lamps *cooperatively* (asking towns to share) unlocks more than hoarding-style fixes.
- **Rewards:**
  - **4 lamps:** **Lamplighter's Boon** — faster wellstone recharge for party gear; grateful towns offer rest-and-resupply.
  - **8 lamps (all):** **The Shared Light** — a permanent party aura that weakens nearby Hollow (the world is warmer where you've been); and a strong narrative push toward **Ending A ("The Shared Dawn")**, since you've literally practiced sharing aether across the sky.
- **Buildable note:** Each lamp is an interaction flag + a small reused encounter; the map glow is a state-driven tint. Cleanly scoped.

## Continuity & scope notes (for the Editor)
- **Canon respected:** no free aether (lamps are rebalanced/stretched, never magically refilled); Resonants are born not made; the Cinderworks quest (SQ-08) reuses the Ascendancy-rigs-burn-out-Resonants ruling as Marrow's gentle echo; the Skyborn-departed mystery is *planted, never settled* (SQ-03, SQ-06, CQ-BRAMBLE) and only the endings resolve it.
- **Ending hooks:** MA-01 (full Song) and CQ-BRAMBLE *accompany and support* **Ending D** as flavor — the **authoritative unlock lives only in `04`'s resolver** (`WARDEN_TRUTH_WHOLE AND ROOKWISE_RECRUITED AND MARROW_REDEEMED`); these quests are not hard gates. MA-02 (shared light) nudges **Ending A**; SQ-05/CQ-MIRA feed the Stillmere expose-vs-protect branch; SQ-08/CQ-KESTREL feed the Ascendancy/Kestrel branches.
- **Revisitable skylands (timing rule):** All eight skylands are freely revisitable by ship for *side content* from when first reached; only the **main-quest** Stillmere reservoir/secret/gate-key beat gates at **A3-07** (`STILLMERE_UNLOCKED`). So Act II Stillmere-adjacent side content (CQ-MIRA, SQ-05, the Stillmere MA-01 fragment) is consistent, and CQ-WREN's Act III return to Meadowmoor follows the same global "side content stays revisitable" rule.
- **Tone:** all loss is "the Long Quiet" / fading to light; no gore; antagonists are frightened or grieving, never cruel; every quest offers a kind, non-violent best answer.
- **Buildability:** all quests reuse the eight canon skylands and existing systems (Listen/Steady/Shape, Hollow encounters, collectible flags, ship travel). No new maps or mechanics are required beyond what the brief and world bible already establish.
